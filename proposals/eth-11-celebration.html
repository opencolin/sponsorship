What a partner gets

We don't sell Gold/Silver/Bronze. You don't buy a bundle to get the one thing you want. We start from your goal and build the right activation around it.

If your goal is developer adoption

Get builders to try your thing in the room: a hands-on demo station, a "build with us" mini-challenge, your tool as the official stack of the night, or a 5-minute lightning demo to the whole room.

If your goal is qualified leads

A reason for builders to share who they are: a raffle or onchain claim that captures verified attendee info, a curated intro session, or a post-event warm list of opted-in attendees who engaged with your activation.

If your goal is brand love with the builder crowd

Be the reason the night is good, not the logo on the wall: host the bar or the toast to 11 years, sponsor the thing people remember (the cake, the commemorative onchain mint, the after-party), put a respected voice from your team on the mic.

If your goal is recruiting

This is a room full of people who can build anything: a "we're hiring" moment, a talent corner, or facilitated intros to engineers matched to your stack.

Every item above is an activation — something that produces a result you can measure — not a logo placement. We agree the target with you up front (signups, demos, leads, intros) and report the actuals within a week.

Sample activations

  1. "Built on Ethereum" onchain mint — every attendee mints a free commemorative 11th-birthday token at the door; the partner is the official mint sponsor and receives the opted-in engagement data. Audience gets a keepsake; partner gets verified, high-intent contact.
  2. The Builder Challenge — a partner-sponsored mini-bounty ("ship the best thing on our API/testnet tonight"), judged live, prize on stage. Audience gets a reason to build; partner gets demos and a leaderboard of engaged engineers.
  3. The Toast to 11 Years — the partner hosts the moment the whole room stops for: a short toast, a great photo, the thing that ends up on everyone's timeline. Audience gets the memory; partner gets the brand association a banner can't buy.
